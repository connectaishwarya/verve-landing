The problem

Most dating apps
weren't built for you

If you work long hours and live at a serious pace, you already know the friction. Existing apps are built for volume, not fit.

01
Lifestyle mismatch
Volume-based swiping surfaces people who look compatible on paper but live completely differently. A match who can't understand a 7pm deadline call isn't really a match.
02
Scheduling friction
Coordinating a first date through chat is surprisingly painful. Most conversations die before a time is ever agreed on. The calendar is the problem nobody solved.
03
Conversations that stall
After a long workday, nobody has the energy to craft thoughtful messages from scratch. Ghosting usually isn't malice — it's exhaustion.
04
Privacy concerns
Sharing a phone number to move to video feels premature. Most apps push you off-platform before real trust is built. Professionals, in particular, notice this.
How it works

From signup to
first date in minutes

1
Set up your professional profile
Quick signup with your job title, career stage, industry, and the windows in your week when you're actually free. No long questionnaires. Five minutes, and you're in.
2
Get matched by lifestyle, not just looks
Verve surfaces people who are compatible in how they live — career stage, pace, values, and availability — not just profile photos and witty bios.
3
Chat — with optional AI assistance
Text chat comes standard. If you want a nudge to keep things interesting, optional paid AI prompts help you stay thoughtful without overthinking it.
4
Schedule a date in one tap
No back-and-forth. Verve reads your availability and suggests realistic time slots — 15 to 30 minutes, virtual or coffee. Both confirm, and it's done.
5
Meet — on your terms, securely
In-app video calls with mutual consent unlock. No phone numbers shared. No pressure to move off-platform before you're ready.
